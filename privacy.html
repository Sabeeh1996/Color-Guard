üíæData Storage

What We Store Locally

ColorGuard stores the following settings on your device using Chrome's local storage API (chrome.storage.sync):

  • Accessibility Mode: Your selected mode (Off, High-Contrast, Hue-Shift, or Edge-Highlight)
  • Mode Settings: Contrast level, hue shift amount, and edge outline thickness
  • Site Preferences: Your whitelist and blacklist of domains
  • General Settings: Whether global mode is enabled and click capture preference

Where Is Data Stored?

All settings are stored locally in your Chrome browser using chrome.storage.sync. This means:

  • ‚úì Data stays on your device
  • ‚úì Data syncs across your Chrome browsers (if Chrome sync is enabled)
  • ‚úì Data is managed by Google's Chrome sync service (not by us)
  • ‚úì You can delete all data by uninstalling the extension

üîÑChrome Sync

If you have Chrome sync enabled in your browser settings, your ColorGuard preferences will sync across your Chrome browsers. This is handled entirely by Google's Chrome sync service:

  • We don't control or have access to this sync mechanism
  • Data is encrypted by Google during sync
  • You can disable sync in Chrome settings
  • Refer to Google's Privacy Policy for details on Chrome sync
